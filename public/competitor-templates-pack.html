What each one borrows from its source

  1. Cascade (Zety) — colored sidebar (1/3) with the name in big white type, skill bars, gold accents. The signature "premium paid resume builder" look. zety.com
  2. Stockholm (Resume.io) — top color band with avatar & role title, two-column body where the narrow left column holds details + skill bars. resume.io
  3. Athena (Novoresume) — circular avatar, burgundy/wine accent, timeline-dot experience entries, proficiency meters. novoresume.com
  4. Crisp (Enhancv) — soft pastel right-column background, creative "My strengths" cards, chip tag rows, icon-lead section heads. enhancv.com
  5. Modernist (Canva) — oversize bold name, geometric shape accents in the header, designer-feel sans-serif (Poppins). canva.com
  6. Aspire (Kickresume / Teal) — black header bar with two-color name, green accent, startup-y sans (Montserrat), light right-column. kickresume.com

How to view

Run npm run dev from resuscore/ and open http://localhost:3000/competitor-templates-pack.html. Or just double-click the file in resuscore/public/ in any browser.

ATS warning on these

The competitor builders all claim "ATS-friendly," but sidebar/two-column layouts do drop parser accuracy on stricter ATS (Workday, Taleo). Industry tests show single-column Jake's-style templates parse at 98–100%, while two-column with colored sidebars drop to ~75–90% on the same ATS. For JobScorer's IT-fresher target audience, I'd recommend offering both — sidebar designs for screening rounds + a single-column "safe" toggle for ATS-heavy applications.

Wiring into your app

To use any of these in JobScorer, each needs a @react-pdf/renderer port under resuscore/src/components/ResumeRenderer/, then registered in resuscore/src/templates/catalog.ts (which currently lists 4 templates). Tell me which 1–3 you want me to port first — Cascade and Stockholm are the highest-converting on competitor sites.
